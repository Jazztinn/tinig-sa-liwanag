# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Sugidanon serves hackathon judges, speech/NLP researchers, Philippine-language technologists, dataset contributors, and community stakeholders who need to understand whether the benchmark is credible, reproducible, and ethically handled. Most arrive with limited context about Hiligaynon ASR, so the experience must explain the finding quickly, then expose enough protocol detail for technical trust.

## Product Purpose

Sugidanon is an open code-switch ASR benchmark for Hiligaynon (Ilonggo). Its primary job is to make matrix-language erasure measurable: current ASR models hear borrowed English and Tagalog words better than the Hiligaynon surrounding them. Success means a visitor can grasp the negative switch penalty, reproduce the numbers, inspect clips and token-level errors, and understand that translation, G2P, and TTS are extension layers rather than the judged artifact.

## Brand Personality

Careful, evidential, community-rooted. The voice should feel like a public research exhibit: plain enough for judges, exact enough for researchers, and respectful toward native speakers whose review and recordings make the corpus possible. The product should communicate urgency without sensationalism.

## Anti-references

Do not present Sugidanon as a finished translation app, consumer voice product, or generic AI demo. Do not blend extension cohorts into the headline result. Do not overclaim Hiligaynon language authority where labels remain `seed_unverified`. Avoid SaaS hero cliches, decorative metric cards that bury methodology, generic "AI for good" framing, and visual treatments that make the benchmark feel less rigorous than the data.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the benchmark, not the scaffold. Every first-screen and summary treatment should foreground ASR evaluation and switch-region WER before translation or downstream possibilities.
2. Make the finding inspectable. Pair headline numbers with clip audio, token diffs, cohort separation, and reproduction links so trust comes from evidence rather than persuasion.
3. Protect subset boundaries. Headline, development, and robustness cohorts must stay visibly separate wherever metrics appear.
4. Use plain language without flattening precision. Judges should understand the story; researchers should still see exact metric definitions, model settings, and caveats.
5. Credit community authorship. Native speakers are authors and reviewers, not anonymous raw data sources.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Maintain strong contrast for all metric text, token states, labels, and chart bars; color must never be the only way to distinguish correctness, errors, or switch regions. Keyboard users must be able to operate filters, clip rows, links, and audio controls. Motion should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`, and glass/transparency effects need solid fallbacks for readability.
